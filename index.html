Executive Brief: Turning “Trustworthy AI” into audit-ready evidence.

From Principles to Proof: The Engineering Standard for Auditable AI.

IEEE P7022 defines the ‘SHALL’ requirements that translate ‘Trustworthy AI’ principles into demonstrable engineering evidence for regulatory and liability defense.

* Formal IEEE SA Working Group participation begins only after NesCom approval and chartering.

Conformance criteria for insurance underwriting Forensic audit trails Agentic system traceability

Board-Level 30-second Summary

The Risk: Current AI governance frameworks are largely process-based; P7022 adds the engineering evidence layer required for auditability and defensibility.
The Solution: P7022 standardizes the Forensic Artifacts (logs, metadata). This enables auditable defense, potentially reducing liability insurance premiums and ensuring regulatory defensibility.

Conformance Evidence Bundle The Output: The actual files handed to the auditor.

Technical Focus: Forensic Evidence

Moving beyond "accuracy metrics" to forensic logs of failures. P7022 standardizes the schema for failure containment, allowing auditors to verify control efficacy.

Scope & Timeline

Standardization Scope

  • Taxonomy: Definitions for failure modes (unsupported claims, source amnesia).
  • Measurement: Engineering metrics for grounding and fidelity.
  • Controls: Technical guardrails (input/output/process).
  • Evidence: The artifact schema for audit.

Intended Domains: ERP, CRM, HRIS, Finance, Ops.

Projected Timeline

  • Mar 2026: NesCom Vote (Pending)
  • Apr/May 2026: Working Group Kickoff
  • 2026–2028: Drafting & pilot implementations (use-case driven)
  • Jun 2028: Initial Ballot
  • Dec 2028: RevCom Approval
